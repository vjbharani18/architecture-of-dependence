# Mentor Questions

## 1. Pravahanadi

Does the Dvaita tradition use *pravahanadi* as the technical term for the beginningless stream of karma/samsara?

Is *sampravaha anadi* ever used technically?

## 2. Anubhava

Can *anubhava* be treated as intrinsic to the jiva?

If yes, in what sense?

If no, what term is more precise?

## 3. Svarupa

How should *svarupa* be distinguished from *svabhava*, *guna*, and *dharma*?

Does the soul's individuality rest on *svarupa* alone?

## 4. Suffering

Is *duhkha* treated as a positive experience, an absence, a relational condition, or something else in Dvaita?

Can a liberated soul be said to have genuinely experienced something contrary to its eternal nature during samsara?

## 5. Bliss

Is liberated bliss intrinsic to the soul, dependent on God, or both in different senses?

How should one distinguish temporary sukha from moksha-sukha?

## 6. Tamo-yogya

If a tamo-yogya jiva seeks happiness, is the object of its seeking structurally different from that of a mukti-yogya jiva?

Can subjective satisfaction coexist with objective suffering?

## 7. Knowledge and Experience

Does the tradition distinguish knowing, experiencing, self-awareness, and recognition in the way this manuscript is beginning to distinguish them?
