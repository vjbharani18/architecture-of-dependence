# Why this chapter exists

This chapter is intentionally different from every chapter that follows.

It is not written as a dialogue.

It serves as the phenomenological starting point of the enquiry.

The reader is not yet evaluating philosophical systems.

The reader is discovering the question that made such systems worth investigating.

The dialogue format begins in Chapter Two.

---

# Source Classification

Historical Basis:

High.

This chapter is directly based on the author's own enquiry and conversations.

Primary inspirations include:

* The self-enquiry exercise discussed by Swami Sarvapriyananda.
* Early curiosity regarding Advaita, Dvaita and Viśiṣṭādvaita.
* The author's own reflections on observing body, mind and thought.

This chapter is therefore largely historical rather than editorial reconstruction.

---

# Editorial Decisions

The sentence

> "The witness."

is intentionally left as a short isolated paragraph.

It reflects the feeling that the enquiry had momentarily reached its conclusion.

The following paragraph immediately destabilises that conclusion through the problem of multiplicity.

This transition establishes the central movement of the entire manuscript.
