# Chapter Thirteen

# Where Does Happiness Actually Exist?

The previous chapter ended with a question.

If happiness and suffering are both experiences,

where does happiness actually exist?

---

## Question

If happiness belongs to the object,

why do different people experience the same object differently?

---

One person delights in music.

Another finds it distracting.

One seeks wealth.

Another willingly gives it away.

One longs for solitude.

Another fears it.

The object remains the same.

The experience changes.

The object alone did not appear sufficient to explain happiness.

---

## Question

Does happiness belong to the mind?

---

The mind certainly participates.

Yet the mind itself is restless.

What satisfied me yesterday

may leave me indifferent tomorrow.

If happiness belongs entirely to the mind,

why does the mind never stop searching?

Perhaps the mind experiences happiness.

Perhaps it does not produce it.

The question remained open.

---

## Question

Does happiness belong to the soul?

---

If happiness belongs intrinsically to the soul,

another difficulty immediately appears.

Why do I not experience it all the time?

Can something intrinsic disappear?

If it cannot,

what exactly is absent?

If it can,

was it truly intrinsic?

The enquiry once again returned to the distinction between

what belongs to a thing

and

what merely becomes manifest.

The answer was not yet clear.

The question remained.

---

## Next Question

Can something exist,

yet remain unexperienced?

If so,

what does it mean for something to exist but not be manifest?
