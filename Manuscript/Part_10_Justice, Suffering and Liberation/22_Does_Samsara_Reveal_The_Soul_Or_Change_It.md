# Chapter Twenty-Two

# Does Saṃsāra Reveal the Soul or Change It?

The previous chapter ended with a question.

If souls possess different **svabhāvas**,

what exactly is the purpose of saṃsāra?

---

## Question

Does saṃsāra change the soul?

Or does it merely reveal what the soul already is?

---

Both possibilities appeared significant.

If saṃsāra changes the intrinsic nature of the soul,

then **svabhāva** itself no longer appears intrinsic.

But if saṃsāra never changes the soul,

another question immediately appears.

---

## Question

Then why does saṃsāra exist at all?

---

Perhaps saṃsāra is not primarily a place of transformation.

Perhaps it is a place of manifestation.

A soul acts.

Experiences.

Chooses.

Responds.

Yet through those actions,

something deeper gradually becomes visible.

Not to God.

But to the soul itself.

---

## Question

If embodiment merely manifests what already exists,

does suffering transform the soul?

Or does suffering reveal the soul?

---

That distinction appeared important.

One suggests change.

The other suggests disclosure.

The enquiry remained open.

---

## Question

If liberation is attained,

has the soul become something new?

Or has its intrinsic nature simply become fully manifest?

---

The question had quietly returned to the same place.

Manifestation.

The idea appeared once again.

Not through physics.

Not through karma.

But through liberation itself.

---

## Next Question

If God is perfectly just,

why should any soul ever experience what appears contrary to its own intrinsic nature?
