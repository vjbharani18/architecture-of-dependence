# Chapter Seven

# What Is the Soul Before It Knows?

Until now,

the enquiry had quietly assumed something.

There is a soul.

The soul seeks.

The soul knows.

The soul suffers.

The soul rejoices.

The soul attains liberation.

But another question now appeared.

---

## Question

Before asking what happens to the soul,

shouldn't I first ask,

**what the soul itself is?**

---

Every explanation I had encountered seemed to speak about what the soul experiences.

Knowledge.

Happiness.

Suffering.

Liberation.

But none of these answered a simpler question.

---

## Question

If knowledge disappeared,

if happiness disappeared,

if suffering disappeared,

what would remain that could still be called the soul?

---

At first,

the answer appeared straightforward.

The soul would still remain.

Experiences come and go.

The soul persists.

But that answer immediately created another question.

---

## Question

If every experience can disappear,

what actually belongs to the soul?

What remains even when everything else is removed?

---

One possibility gradually emerged.

Perhaps happiness does not belong intrinsically.

Perhaps suffering does not belong intrinsically.

Perhaps even knowledge, in its fullest manifestation, does not belong intrinsically.

Then what does?

I found myself reducing the question further.

Not,

"What does the soul experience?"

But,

"What can never be removed from the soul?"

That question became the centre of the enquiry.

---

## Next Question

If I keep removing everything that appears accidental,

what finally belongs to the soul?
