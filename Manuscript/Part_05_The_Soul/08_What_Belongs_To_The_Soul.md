# Chapter Eight

# What Belongs to the Soul?

The previous chapter ended with a simple question.

If everything accidental is removed,

what finally belongs to the soul?

---

## Question

Does happiness belong to the soul?

Or is happiness something the soul experiences?

---

Happiness appeared to vary.

Sometimes it was present.

Sometimes it was absent.

Something that comes and goes did not immediately appear to belong to the soul's permanent nature.

The same question naturally arose again.

---

## Question

Does suffering belong to the soul?

Or is suffering something the soul experiences?

---

The pattern seemed similar.

Suffering also appeared to come and go.

If suffering can disappear,

perhaps it too is not intrinsic.

Then another possibility appeared.

---

## Question

Perhaps knowledge belongs intrinsically?

---

That too seemed difficult.

Knowledge appears capable of increase.

Learning occurs.

Ignorance disappears.

Liberation is often spoken of as fuller knowledge.

If knowledge can become fuller,

does knowledge itself belong to the immutable nature of the soul?

Or does only the capacity remain?

The enquiry continued.

---

## Question

Is experience the only thing that truly belongs to the soul?

Not happiness.

Not suffering.

Not even knowledge in its fullest manifestation.

Perhaps the soul is fundamentally the experiencer.

The one to whom all experiences belong.

For a while,

this appeared elegant.

Then another question emerged almost immediately.

---

## Question

If every soul is fundamentally just an experiencer,

why are souls different?

Have I answered the question?

Or have I simply moved the difficulty somewhere else?

---

The enquiry had not reached an answer.

It had only reached another boundary.

---
