# Chapter Seventeen

# If God Can Grant Liberation, Why Not Liberate Every Soul?

The previous chapter left another question unresolved.

If God possesses complete knowledge,

complete power,

and perfect compassion,

why does suffering continue?

The question gradually became more specific.

---

## Question

If every soul seeks happiness,

why doesn't God simply grant liberation to every soul?

---

One response I encountered distinguished

God's compassion

from

the intrinsic nature of souls.

According to the Dvaita tradition,

souls are not merely different because of their experiences.

They differ intrinsically.

This eventually led me to the doctrine of

**mukti-yogyatva**

and the threefold classification of souls.

I was less interested in the terminology

than in the question it was trying to answer.

---

## Question

If every soul seeks happiness,

isn't liberation what every soul ultimately wants?

---

The response surprised me.

Perhaps not every soul seeks the same happiness.

Perhaps what appears as fulfilment to one soul

does not appear as fulfilment to another.

If that is true,

then the question is no longer simply about liberation.

It becomes a question about desire itself.

---

## Question

If a soul rejects God,

is it rejecting happiness?

Or is it experiencing something else as happiness?

---

This seemed to move the discussion to a deeper level.

Perhaps the disagreement was not about knowledge.

Perhaps it was about the very nature of what different souls seek.

I found the question difficult,

not because I had rejected the answer,

but because I wanted to understand exactly what it meant.

---

## Question

If different souls ultimately seek different ends,

does justice require that every soul receive the same destiny?

Or does justice consist in every soul reaching the end consistent with its own intrinsic nature?

---

The question remained open.

It also became one of the deepest questions in the enquiry.

---

## Next Question

If souls differ intrinsically,

what exactly is the nature of that intrinsic difference?
