# Chapter Sixteen

# If God Already Knows Every Choice, Am I Truly Free?

The previous chapter ended with a question.

If God perfectly knows Himself,

what does it mean for God to know everything else?

One question immediately followed.

---

## Question

If God already knows every choice I will ever make,

am I genuinely free?

---

At first,

the difficulty appeared obvious.

If every future choice is already known,

doesn't that make the future fixed?

And if the future is fixed,

where is freedom?

One response distinguished

knowledge

from

causation.

Knowing an event does not necessarily cause the event.

That seemed reasonable.

Yet another question immediately appeared.

---

## Question

The problem isn't simply that God knows.

The problem is that God knows **before** I choose.

Doesn't that make all the difference?

---

The more I thought about it,

the more another possibility emerged.

Perhaps the word **before**

already assumes that God exists within time.

If God is not another being moving through moments as I do,

perhaps "before" and "after" cannot be applied in the same way.

That did not answer the question.

It only made me realise that I might be asking a question about time,

while believing I was asking a question about freedom.

---

## Question

If God stands outside time,

what does "the future" even mean from God's perspective?

---

At this point,

I realised I did not yet understand time well enough to answer the original question.

Rather than forcing an answer,

I left the question open.

---

## Next Question

If God possesses the power to liberate,

why are all souls not liberated?
