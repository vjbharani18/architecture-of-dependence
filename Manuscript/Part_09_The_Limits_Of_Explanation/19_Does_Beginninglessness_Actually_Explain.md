# Chapter Nineteen

# Does Beginninglessness Actually Explain?

The previous chapter ended with another question.

If the relation between soul and karma is beginningless,

what exactly has been explained?

---

## Question

What does **pravāhānādi** actually explain?

---

During my own enquiry, I first referred to this idea as *sampravaha anadi*. Later, I realised that the traditional terminology required verification. What interested me, however, was never the terminology itself.

It was the explanatory role of the idea.

---

## Question

Does beginninglessness explain why souls differ?

Or does it only explain why there is no first beginning?

---

The more I thought about it,

the more another comparison appeared.

Earlier,

I had encountered the word

*anirvacanīya.*

Now,

I was encountering

*pravāhānādi.*

The traditions were different.

The terminology was different.

Yet I found myself asking a similar question of both.

---

## Question

How is this different from *anirvacanīya*?

Have I reached two different traditions,

each arriving at a point where explanation no longer proceeds?

Or are they doing fundamentally different philosophical work?

---

I resisted assuming they were the same.

They belong to different metaphysical systems.

Yet the comparison itself appeared unavoidable.

The enquiry therefore became more careful.

I was no longer comparing doctrines.

I was comparing the logical role each doctrine played.

---

## Question

When should I say,

"I now understand the explanation,"

rather than,

"I have learned a new word"?

---

That question remained unanswered.

It also became one of the most important questions in the entire enquiry.

---
