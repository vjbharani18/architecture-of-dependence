# Chapter Eighteen

# How Does Beginningless Karma Become *My* Karma?

The previous chapter ended with a question about the intrinsic nature of souls.

As I tried to understand the Dvaita explanation, another question gradually became unavoidable.

---

## Question

If karma is beginningless,

how does it become **my** karma?

---

The soul and karma appear to be distinct.

One is the experiencer.

The other determines the experiences that unfold.

That immediately led me to another question.

---

## Question

If the soul and karma are different entities,

how did the relation between them arise?

---

Several possibilities appeared.

If God established the relation,

then the relation itself appears to have a beginning.

If the relation has a beginning,

in what sense is karma beginningless?

Another possibility seemed to be that no one established the relation.

But that immediately raised another question.

---

## Question

If nobody established the relation,

how does the relation exist at all?

---

Perhaps the relation itself is beginningless.

That appeared to answer one difficulty.

Yet another immediately appeared.

---

## Question

If the relation itself is beginningless,

have I explained the relation?

Or have I merely said that it has always existed?

---

The question did not arise because I doubted the tradition.

It arose because I wanted to understand exactly what the explanation was claiming.

The enquiry had slowly shifted.

I was no longer asking,

*"What is karma?"*

I was asking,

*"What explanatory work is the idea of beginninglessness actually doing?"*

---
