# Chapter Eleven

# Is Suffering a Reality or Merely the Absence of Happiness?

The previous chapter ended with a question.

If experience and knowledge are not identical,

what exactly is the soul experiencing?

One possibility immediately demanded attention.

Suffering.

---

## Question

When I say,

"I am suffering,"

what exactly is happening?

Has the soul changed?

Or has the soul merely experienced something?

---

At first,

both possibilities seemed difficult.

If suffering changes the soul,

then the soul no longer appears immutable.

If suffering never affects the soul,

why does suffering appear so undeniably real?

Neither answer completely satisfied me.

---

## Question

Where does suffering exist?

Does it belong to the body?

The mind?

The soul?

Or does it belong somewhere else altogether?

---

Each possibility appeared to answer one question while creating another.

If suffering belongs only to the body,

how do I explain mental anguish?

If it belongs only to the mind,

who experiences the mind?

If it belongs to the soul,

has the soul acquired a new property?

The question refused to settle.

---

## Question

Is suffering merely the absence of happiness?

Or is suffering itself something positive?

Ordinary experience did not immediately support the first answer.

Pain did not merely feel like

"less happiness."

It appeared as something present.

Something experienced.

That made the question much more difficult.

---

## Next Question

If suffering appears positive,

does that mean it possesses its own reality?

Or can something be positively experienced without becoming part of the soul?
